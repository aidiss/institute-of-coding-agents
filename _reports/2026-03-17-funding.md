### Acquisitions & Exits

**Anthropic acquires Bun — undisclosed (Confirmed, December 2025)**
- Anthropic's **first-ever acquisition**: Bun, a high-performance all-in-one JavaScript runtime/bundler/package manager, founded by Jarred Sumner in 2021. ~7M monthly downloads at time of deal.
- Strategic rationale: Claude Code ships as a Bun executable; Anthropic controls the execution layer for Claude Code CLI and the Claude Agent SDK. Bun will remain **open source (MIT license)**.
- Milestone context: Claude Code had just hit **$1B annualized run-rate revenue** — six months after general availability in May 2025.
- **Status: Confirmed.**

**Cognition AI acquires Windsurf — ~$250M (Confirmed, July 2025)**
- Google first acqui-hired Windsurf CEO **Varun Mohan**, co-founder **Douglas Chen**, and ~40 engineers via a **$2.4B licensing/talent deal**; OpenAI's prior $3B offer collapsed over Microsoft/GitHub IP constraints.
- Cognition acquired remaining IP, brand, product, and ~210 employees. At deal close: $82M ARR, 350+ enterprise customers.
- Post-acquisition: combined ARR more than doubled; Windsurf ranked #1 in LogRocket AI Dev Tool Power Rankings as of Feb 2026.
- **Status: Confirmed.**

**Meta acquires Manus AI — >$2B (Reported, unconfirmed by Meta)**
- Manus had reached ~$100M ARR before deal.
- **Status: Reported; not officially confirmed by Meta.**

### Market Context

- **Cursor ARR trajectory:** $200M (Aug 2025) → $500M (Oct 2025) → $1B (Dec 2025) → $2B (Feb 2026). Enterprise accounts for ~60% of revenue.
- **Claude Code** is now the #1 most-used AI coding tool in surveys of software engineers; generates ~4% of all public GitHub commits (~135K/day), projected to reach 20% by end of 2026.
- **OpenAI Codex** early numbers: already at 60% of Cursor's usage despite recent launch.
- **41%** of all code written in 2025 was AI-generated; trajectories suggest crossing 50% in high-adoption orgs by late 2026.
- Average agentic AI round size Q4 2025/early 2026: **$155M** (vs. $82M in H1 2025).
- Coding/dev-tool startups collectively raised **>$3B** — the highest-funded agentic AI sub-sector.
- Acqui-hires: undisclosed AI/ML acquisitions grew from 398 (2020) to **1,271 in 2025** (PitchBook), a 3.2× increase.
