### Notes

- **Anysphere (Cursor)** has crossed $2B in annualized recurring revenue as of April 2026, up from $1B in late 2025 and $500M in June 2025. The company is in discussions to raise a new round of up to $5B at a valuation between $50B and $60B — roughly double the $29.3B Series D price set just four months earlier (Nov 2025). No close confirmed.
- **Replit** confirmed its $400M Series D, previously reported in progress at the time of the March 7 report. The $9B valuation is a 3× step-up from the $3B prior round and reflects $240M+ in 2025 revenue and 40M total users. Replit is targeting $1B ARR by end of 2026.
- **Entire** is a new developer-infrastructure platform founded by former GitHub CEO Nat Friedman. The $60M seed round is one of the largest on record for a developer tools startup at that stage. The product extends Git to capture structured AI agent session metadata (prompts, token usage, tool calls), positioning the platform as a software-development-lifecycle layer for human–agent collaboration.
- **Emergent**, the India-based vibe-coding platform, tripled its valuation from ~$100M to $300M in just one round. The company claims $50M ARR, 5M+ users across 190 countries, and was targeting $100M ARR by April 2026.

## Acquisitions & Exits

### Anthropic / Bun — Strategic Acquisition (Dec 2025, Confirmed)

Anthropic acquired Bun, the open-source JavaScript/TypeScript runtime and all-in-one developer toolkit (runtime, package manager, bundler, test runner), in its **first-ever acquisition**. The announcement coincided with Claude Code reaching **$1 billion in annualized run-rate revenue**, just six months after its public launch.

- **Founder:** Jarred Sumner (2021); Bun reports 7M+ monthly downloads and top usage on OpenRouter
- **Strategic rationale:** Anthropic is betting on Bun as the execution substrate for Claude Code, the Claude Agent SDK, and future AI coding products. The acquisition secures control of the runtime layer, enabling Anthropic to build Constitutional AI guardrails and safety constraints into execution itself, not just the generation step.
- **Terms:** Bun will remain open source and MIT-licensed. Financial terms not disclosed.

This deal follows the pattern identified in prior reports: major labs are acquiring **infrastructure** rather than competing LLMs, targeting the toolchain layers where AI-generated code actually runs.

## Talent Moves

### Yann LeCun — Meta → AMI Labs (Nov 2025 departure; startup unveiled Jan 2026)

Yann LeCun, Meta's Chief AI Scientist for over a decade, departed Meta and founded **Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (AMI Labs)**, targeting a **$3B valuation** at launch. LeCun's departure followed:

1. Meta's ~$14B acquisition of a 49% stake in Scale AI and the appointment of its 28-year-old founder Alexandr Wang to lead Meta's superintelligence team — which caused LeCun to begin reporting to Wang rather than directly to Meta's CPO.
2. Persistent internal tension over Zuckerberg's commercialization push vs. LeCun's open-ended research agenda.
3. The public failure of Llama 4, which was widely criticized at release.

AMI Labs is focused on **world-model architectures** — models trained to learn from video and spatial data rather than text tokens — representing LeCun's long-held contrarian bet against the LLM paradigm. LeCun serves as Executive Chair.

### Nat Friedman — Investor → Entire (Feb 2026)

Former GitHub CEO Nat Friedman transitioned from active investor to founder with the launch of **Entire** (see Deals above). The $60M seed is notable not just for its size but for the founding thesis: Friedman argues that Git, issues, and pull requests were designed for human-to-human collaboration and are structurally unfit for a world where AI agents are the primary code producers.

### Sid Sijbrandij — GitLab → Kilo Code (co-founder, seed 2025)

GitLab co-founder and Executive Chair Sid Sijbrandij co-founded **Kilo Code**, an open-source, model-agnostic VS Code extension. (Covered in the March 7 deals table; noted here for context on the talent move.) Kilo Code raised $8M seed led by Cota Capital with General Catalyst, Breakers, Quiet Capital, and Tokyo Black. The platform now processes 6.1T tokens/month with 750K+ downloads.

## Context

The AI coding agent funding market has entered a hypergrowth phase with clear signs of concentration at the top:

- **Cursor** is pulling away: $2B ARR at a potential $60B valuation implies a ~30× revenue multiple, while the company doubles ARR every three months.
- **Replit** ($9B) and **Lovable** ($6.6B, prior report) have locked in their positions as the second tier.
- **New entrant capital** continues to flow in at the seed level (Entire, Emergent), but the gap to the leaders is widening.
- The **Anthropic / Bun** acquisition has established a new deal archetype: buying the execution layer rather than the application layer.
- **Talent gravity** is now pulling senior researchers (LeCun, Friedman, Sijbrandij) out of BigTech and late-stage companies into founding roles — a signal that the space is still early enough for platform bets, despite the valuations.
