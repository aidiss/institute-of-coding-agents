### Notes

- **Anysphere (Cursor)** is in active talks for a round of up to $5B at a $60B valuation as of early April 2026. The company surpassed $2B in annualized revenue in March 2026, up from $500M ARR just nine months prior. Fortune 500 enterprise clients now represent 60% of revenue. Previous round: $2.3B Series D at $29.3B (Nov 2025), led by Accel and Coatue, with NVIDIA and Google as strategic investors. The potential $60B figure would represent a 2× step-up in under six months — the fastest valuation acceleration in developer-tools history.
- **Replit** confirmed its $400M Series D in March 2026, tripling its valuation from $3B (Sep 2025) in six months. Growth driven by agentic AI features and a shift to consumption-based pricing. Reported $240M+ in 2025 revenue; targeting $1B ARR by end of 2026. The company has 40M+ total users.
- **Cognition AI** raised $400M led by Founders Fund at a $10.2B post-money valuation (Sep 2025), up from ~$4B earlier in 2025. This was the first round after its acquisition of Windsurf; combined ARR rose 30%+ in the seven weeks post-acquisition. Enterprise anchor customers include Goldman Sachs, Citi, Palantir, Ramp, and Nubank.

## Acquisitions & Exits

### Nvidia / Groq — $20B Licensing + Acqui-Hire (Dec 24, 2025, Confirmed)

Nvidia structured a $20 billion transaction to license Groq's Language Processing Unit (LPU) inference architecture and simultaneously hire founder/CEO Jonathan Ross, President Sunny Madra, and the majority of Groq's engineering team. Groq technically remains an independent entity under new CEO Simon Edwards and retains GroqCloud operations and IP. Critics and regulators describe the arrangement as an acqui-hire disguised as a licensing deal to avoid merger review. The FTC, emboldened by its Microsoft-Inflection AI investigation, is scrutinizing the deal. Ross now serves as SVP of Inference Architecture at Nvidia.

### Meta / Dreamer AI — Acqui-Hire (Mar 24, 2026, Confirmed)

Meta hired the entire team of Dreamer AI, including all three co-founders, who joined Meta Superintelligence Labs. No acquisition price disclosed. Meta spent $72B on capex in 2025 and has projected up to $135B in 2026; the Dreamer acqui-hire is one of several talent consolidation moves tied to its superintelligence push.

### OpenAI / Alex Codes — Acqui-Hire (Sep 2025, Confirmed)

OpenAI hired the three-person team behind Alex, an AI-powered coding assistant integrated with Apple's Xcode, led by Daniel Edrisian. The team joined OpenAI's Codex division. New downloads of the Alex app were halted following the move. The deal coincided with Apple shipping native AI coding features in Xcode 26.3, reducing the commercial runway for third-party Xcode integrations.

## Context

Q1 2026 set an all-time record with approximately $300B in global venture investment, of which $242B (80%) went to AI. The AI coding agent sub-sector is among the most active, with valuations stretching further from revenue multiples as investors underwrite future platform dominance rather than present earnings. Three structural trends define the current moment:

1. **Acqui-hire as the dominant M&A structure.** Google/Windsurf, Nvidia/Groq, OpenAI/Alex Codes, and Meta/Dreamer all used talent-plus-license or talent-only structures to sidestep full merger review. The FTC under Chair Andrew Ferguson announced in January 2026 it would investigate this pattern industry-wide.
2. **Consumption revenue unlocking growth.** Replit's shift to usage-based agent pricing (late 2024) and Cognition's post-Windsurf enterprise bundling both accelerated ARR faster than seat-license models, providing a template others are copying.
3. **Valuation compression risk.** Cursor at $60B on $2B ARR is a 30× forward multiple; Replit at $9B on a ~$240M 2025 revenue base is 37×. Both bets require continued category expansion. Any sign of ARR stall or competitive commoditization could trigger rapid mark-downs.
