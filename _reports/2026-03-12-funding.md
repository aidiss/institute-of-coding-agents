## Acquisitions & Exits

### Databricks acquires Quotient AI — Confirmed (March 11, 2026)
Databricks acquired **Quotient AI**, an agent evaluation and reinforcement-learning startup built by former GitHub Copilot quality engineers. Quotient monitors agent behavior in production, detects regressions, pinpoints failure root causes, and feeds a continuous RL loop. Terms undisclosed. Announced alongside the launch of **Genie Code**, Databricks' autonomous data-engineering agent; on real-world tasks, Genie Code more than doubled the task-success rate of competing coding agents (32.1% → 77.1%). Quotient's technology will be integrated into Genie, Genie Code, and Agent Bricks.

### OpenAI acquires Promptfoo — Confirmed (March 9, 2026)
OpenAI is acquiring **Promptfoo**, an AI security and red-teaming platform founded in 2024 by Ian Webster and Michael D'Angelo. Total funding: $22.68M (Series A led by Insight Partners; a16z also participated). Promptfoo's tools cover prompt injection, jailbreak detection, data-leak testing, tool misuse, and out-of-policy agent behavior; used by 25%+ of Fortune 500 companies. Post-acquisition, technology integrates into **OpenAI Frontier** (its agentic enterprise platform). OpenAI committed to continuing Promptfoo's open-source offering.

### Anthropic acquires Vercept — Confirmed (Feb 25, 2026)
Anthropic's second-ever acquisition. **Vercept** (Seattle, AI2 spinout) built **Vy**, a cloud-hosted computer-use agent capable of operating a remote macOS machine. Co-founders: Kiana Ehsani, Luca Weihs, and Ross Girshick (computer vision expert, ex-FAIR, ex-Microsoft Research). Total funding: $50M ($16.5M seed in Jan 2025 at $67M valuation; backers included Eric Schmidt, Jeff Dean, Kyle Vogt, Arash Ferdowsi). Terms undisclosed. Co-founder Matt Deitke had already departed mid-2025 when Meta reportedly offered him $250M over four years. Vercept's Vy product shutting down 30 days post-announcement. Acquisition follows Anthropic's December 2025 purchase of **Bun** (JavaScript runtime used to ship Claude Code). Fallout: UiPath (NYSE: PATH) fell 3.6% on announcement day.

### Cognition acquires Windsurf (~$250M, Jul 2025) — Confirmed (standing item)
Following the collapse of OpenAI's $3B deal and Google's $2.4B reverse acquihire of Windsurf leadership, Cognition acquired the remaining IP, brand, product, and ~210 employees. Windsurf had ~$82M ARR at acquisition; combined enterprise ARR grew 30%+ post-deal.

### Meta acquires Manus AI (2026) — Confirmed
Meta acquired AI agent startup **Manus** for a reported $2B+. Manus had approximately $100M ARR at the time.

### OpenAI acquires Windsurf — Collapsed (Failed)
OpenAI's $3B definitive agreement for Windsurf fell apart due to Microsoft IP entanglement. OpenAI has since pivoted to building its own developer-facing IDE and a reported GitHub competitor (*The Information*, Mar 3, 2026).

### Predicted targets (investor commentary)
Factory, Codegen, Warp, Datadog, and Sentry are cited as likely M&A targets. *The Information* published a 2026 prediction that **Microsoft will acquire an AI coding startup**. Datadog has invested in four agent observability players since 2024 (LangChain, Arize, Braintrust, Patronus AI).

## Talent Moves

### Alibaba Qwen AI: leadership exodus — Confirmed (Jan–Mar 2026)
Three senior leaders departed Alibaba's Qwen AI division within ten weeks:
- **Hui Binyuan** (staff research scientist, lead of Qwen Code) → **Meta** (January 2026)
- **Lin Junyang ("Justin Lin")** (key Qwen technical leader) — posted farewell on X on March 3, 2026, one day after Alibaba unified all products under the Qwen brand. No destination announced.
- **Yu Bowen** (head of post-training for Qwen models, overseeing RLHF, instruction tuning, and alignment) — resigned the same week; reported by LatePost. No destination announced.

The absence of announced landing spots for Lin and Yu suggests departures may have been initiated by Alibaba rather than pulled by competing offers.

### Thomas Dohmke — GitHub CEO → Entire founder (Feb 2026)
Former GitHub CEO launched **Entire**, an open-source platform for engineering teams managing AI agent fleets. First product: **Checkpoints** — captures context behind AI-written code (prompts, agent reasoning, decisions). The $60M seed is reportedly the largest ever for a dev tools startup. Backers include Jerry Yang (Yahoo co-founder), Garry Tan (YC CEO), Datadog CEO Olivier Pomel, Gergely Orosz.

### Ross Girshick & Vercept co-founders → Anthropic (Feb 2026)
Computer vision expert **Ross Girshick** (ex-FAIR, ex-Microsoft Research) and Vercept co-founders **Kiana Ehsani** and **Luca Weihs** join Anthropic via the Vercept acquisition to advance Claude's computer-use capabilities.

### Peter Steinberger (OpenClaw creator) → OpenAI Codex team (early 2026)
Creator of **OpenClaw** (fastest-growing open-source AI agent) joined **OpenAI's Codex team** after being courted by every major lab. OpenClaw transferred to a newly formed foundation with Dave Morin on the board.

### Varun Mohan & Douglas Chen — Windsurf → Google DeepMind (Jul 2025)
**Varun Mohan** (CEO) and **Douglas Chen** (co-founder) joined Google DeepMind's Gemini coding team as part of the $2.4B licensing/acquihire. ~40 senior R&D staff moved with them. Google's resulting tool: **Antigravity**, built on Windsurf technology with "Mission Control" for multi-agent orchestration.

### Alan Cowen — Hume AI → Google DeepMind (Jan 2026)
Google DeepMind hired **Alan Cowen** (CEO) and ~7 engineers from voice AI startup **Hume AI**. **Andrew Ettinger** replaced Cowen as Hume AI CEO.

### AWS executive shakeups (early 2026)
- **David Luan** (Head of AGI Lab / Agentic AI R&D) departed AWS to start a new venture.
- **Scott Rosecrans** (VP of AI Sales) → OpenAI.
- **Jigar Thakkar** (ex-Microsoft Teams founding member) → AWS as VP of Agentic AI for Business.

- **U.S. AI investment:** ~$109.1B in private AI funding in 2025 — nearly 12× China ($9.3B). Big Tech expected to invest ~$650B in AI throughout 2026 (Bridgewater Associates).
- **MCP:** Model Context Protocol reached **97M monthly SDK downloads** (Feb 2026). Adopted by OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Amazon. AGENTS.md standard now in 60,000+ open-source projects.
- **Developer adoption:** ~85% of developers use AI coding tools regularly. Claude Code now most-used per survey; AI generates ~30% of Microsoft's code and 25%+ of Google's.
