## Acquisitions & Exits

### Meta acquires Moltbook — Confirmed (Mar 12, 2026)
Meta acquired **Moltbook**, an AI agent network startup. Industry observers characterize the deal primarily as an **acqui-hire** — Meta seeking developers experimenting with AI agent ecosystems rather than Moltbook's bot-focused social product. Terms undisclosed. Talent joining **Meta's Superintelligence Labs**.

### OpenAI acquires Convogo team — Confirmed (Jan 8, 2026; standing item)
OpenAI acqui-hired the team behind **Convogo** (executive coaching automation). All-stock deal; OpenAI acquiring team only, not IP. Convogo's ninth acquisition in a year per PitchBook.

### Microsoft acqui-hires Robin AI tech team — Confirmed (Jan 9, 2026; standing item)
Microsoft is acqui-hiring the tech team of **Robin AI** (legal AI). Team reportedly numbered several dozen; will work on improving Word for lawyers.

### Predicted targets (standing item)
Factory, Codegen, Warp, and Sentry remain cited by investors as likely M&A targets. *The Information* (Mar 3) predicted Microsoft will acquire an AI coding startup in 2026.

## Talent Moves

### Peter Steinberger (OpenClaw) → OpenAI Codex team — Confirmed (early 2026)
Creator of **OpenClaw** — described as the fastest-growing open-source AI coding agent — joined **OpenAI's Codex team** after being recruited by every major AI lab. The move sparked broad discussion: labs have concluded that personal agents are a **strategic control point**, not just a feature, and want to own agent orchestration, not just model inference. OpenClaw transferred to a newly formed foundation; Dave Morin on the board.

### Alibaba Qwen AI: leadership exodus — Confirmed (Jan–Mar 2026; standing item)
Three senior leaders departed within ten weeks:
- **Hui Binyuan** (Qwen Code lead) → **Meta** (January 2026)
- **Lin Junyang ("Justin Lin")** (key Qwen technical leader) — posted farewell on X on March 3; destination unannounced
- **Yu Bowen** (head of post-training; RLHF, instruction tuning, alignment) — resigned same week; destination unannounced

## Market Context

- **Acqui-hire regulatory pressure:** FTC and DOJ tightened review of "license and hire" structures throughout 2025. DOJ opened formal investigation into Google–Character.AI; FTC staff report concluded such "pseudo-acquisitions" may constitute unfair competition. Large tech companies increasingly using all-stock, no-IP structures (e.g., OpenAI–Convogo) to stay below formal review thresholds.
- **Frontier AI researcher scarcity:** Fewer than 40,000 frontier AI researchers globally; demand growing 20% by 2034 (LinkedIn). Companies pay 56% premium for AI skills. Acqui-hires accelerating as a result.
- **AI coding market:** Global AI agent market growing from $7.84B (2025) to a projected $52.6B (2030). ~85% of developers now use AI coding tools regularly; Claude Code leads among tools per a survey of ~1,000 engineers.
