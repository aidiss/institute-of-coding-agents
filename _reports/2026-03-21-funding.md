## Acquisitions & Exits

### OpenAI Acquires Astral (March 19, 2026) — Confirmed
OpenAI announced the acquisition of **Astral**, a Python developer tooling startup founded in 2022 by **Charlie Marsh**. Astral built three widely-used open-source tools: **uv** (dependency/environment management), **Ruff** (fast Rust-based linter and formatter), and **ty** (type safety enforcement). Astral's tools collectively see hundreds of millions of downloads per month. Financial terms were not disclosed. OpenAI intends to integrate Astral's tooling and team into its **Codex** product line. Codex has grown 3× in users and 5× in usage since January 2026, with over 2M weekly active users. Deal is pending regulatory approval. Both parties will remain separate and independent until closing.

The acquisition mirrors Anthropic's December 2025 Bun acquisition in structure — both companies acquired open-source JavaScript/Python infrastructure already embedded in their core coding products. Anthropic's acquisition of Bun in December 2025 specifically prompted OpenAI's Astral move.

OpenAI also recently acquired **Promptfoo** (AI security testing), **OpenClaw**, and closed-source LaTeX platform **Crixet** (rebranded Prism) in recent months.

### The Windsurf Saga (May–July 2025) — Confirmed
The most complex deal sequence of the year:

1. **OpenAI-Windsurf acquisition (~$3B, May 2025) — COLLAPSED:** OpenAI entered exclusivity for a $3B acquisition of Windsurf. Deal collapsed when Microsoft insisted on rights to Windsurf IP under its OpenAI partnership; OpenAI refused, viewing Windsurf's tech as competitive with GitHub Copilot.
2. **Google acqui-hires Windsurf founders (Jul 11, 2025, ~$2.4B total):** Google paid ~$1.2B to investors and ~$1.2B in compensation packages to ~40 employees joining Google DeepMind, including co-founders Varun Mohan and Douglas Chen. Google received a non-exclusive technology license — not a full acquisition. Prior valuation: $3B (May 2025).
3. **Cognition acquires remaining Windsurf entity (Jul 14, 2025):** Cognition acquired Windsurf's IP, product, brand, and remaining ~210 staff. Combined ARR approximately doubled to ~$155M post-acquisition.

### Anthropic Acquires Bun (December 2025) — Confirmed
Anthropic's first-ever acquisition. Bun (JavaScript runtime/toolkit by Jarred Sumner, 7M monthly downloads, 82K GitHub stars) acquired to own the execution layer underlying Claude Code, which ships as a single Bun executable. Terms undisclosed. Bun remains open-source (MIT license).

### Anysphere (Cursor) Acquisitions (2024–2025) — Confirmed
- **Supermaven (Nov 2024):** Founded by Jacob Jackson (ex-Tabnine, ex-OpenAI); 35,000 developers; raised $12M from Bessemer and John Schulman. Acquired to accelerate Tab AI model.
- **Koala (~$129M, Jul 2025):** AI CRM acqui-hire; product shut down Sep 2025.
- **Graphite ("way over" $290M, Dec 2025):** Code review platform serving Shopify, Snowflake, Figma, Perplexity. Continues operating independently.

### Cognition Raises Post-Windsurf (Aug 2025) — Confirmed
Following the Windsurf acquisition, Cognition raised ~$500M at a $9.8B valuation — more than doubling its prior valuation.

- OpenAI responded to talent pressure with up to $1.5M retention bonuses and accelerated vesting.
- Principal AI researchers commanding $1M+/year packages; superstar individuals negotiating tens of millions.

## Market Context

- AI coding tools collectively raised **$3B+** in software development segment alone, making it the highest-funded vertical in agentic AI.
- **Average deal size** in agentic AI: $7.4M (2022) → $26.2M (2023) → $109.9M (2024) → $527.8M (2025).
- **AI-native IDEs** captured 38% of all AI code assistant funding ($3.59B across 8 deals), driven primarily by Cursor and Windsurf.
- **GitHub Copilot** holds ~42% market share of paid AI coding tools; Cursor ~18%.
- **Sequoia** backed at least four AI coding companies: Magic.dev, Sourcegraph, Factory AI, Zed.
- **YC** backed ~30 of 80 funded AI code assistant startups — the most active early-stage pipeline in the market.
- Cursor reached $1B ARR in ~17 months — reportedly the fastest B2B SaaS to reach that milestone in history.
- Global agentic AI market projected to grow from $9.14B (2026) to $139B by 2034.
