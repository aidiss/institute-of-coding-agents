## Acquisitions & Exits

### OpenAI Acquires Astral (March 19, 2026) — Confirmed
OpenAI announced the acquisition of **Astral**, a Python developer tooling startup founded in 2022 by **Charlie Marsh**. Astral built three widely-used open-source tools: **uv** (dependency/environment management), **Ruff** (fast Rust-based linter and formatter), and **ty** (type safety enforcement). Astral's tools collectively see hundreds of millions of downloads per month. All 30+ employees including Marsh will join OpenAI's Codex division. Financial terms undisclosed; tools remain open source. Deal pending regulatory closing.

The acquisition mirrors Anthropic's December 2025 Bun acquisition — both companies acquired open-source infrastructure already embedded in their core coding products, racing to own the full developer toolchain.

OpenAI has also recently acquired **Promptfoo** (AI security testing), **OpenClaw**, and closed-source LaTeX platform **Crixet** (rebranded Prism).

### Lovable Actively Hunting Acquisitions (March 23, 2026) — Confirmed
CEO **Anton Osika** announced on X that Lovable is seeking acquisitions and talent team acqui-hires to accelerate its app-builder position. No specific targets named. The announcement came alongside the company reporting $400M ARR — doubling from $200M at end of 2025.

### The Windsurf Saga (May–July 2025) — Confirmed
The most complex deal sequence of the year:

1. **OpenAI-Windsurf acquisition (~$3B, May 2025) — COLLAPSED:** OpenAI entered exclusivity for a $3B acquisition. Deal collapsed when Microsoft insisted on rights to Windsurf IP under its OpenAI partnership.
2. **Google acqui-hires Windsurf founders (Jul 11, 2025, ~$2.4B):** Google paid ~$1.2B to investors and ~$1.2B in compensation packages to ~40 employees joining Google DeepMind, including co-founders Varun Mohan and Douglas Chen. Non-exclusive technology license — not a full acquisition. Prior valuation: $3B (May 2025).
3. **Cognition acquires remaining Windsurf entity (Jul 14, 2025, ~$250M):** Cognition acquired Windsurf's IP, product, brand, and remaining ~210 staff. Combined ARR approximately doubled to ~$155M post-acquisition. Three weeks later, Cognition laid off 30 employees and offered buyouts to ~200 remaining Windsurf staff.

### Anthropic Acquires Bun (December 2025) — Confirmed
Anthropic's first-ever acquisition. Bun (JavaScript runtime/toolkit by Jarred Sumner; 7M monthly downloads, 82K GitHub stars) acquired to own the execution layer underlying Claude Code, which ships as a single Bun executable. Terms undisclosed. Bun remains open-source (MIT license).

### Anysphere (Cursor) Acquisitions (2024–2025) — Confirmed
- **Supermaven (Nov 2024):** Founded by Jacob Jackson (ex-Tabnine, ex-OpenAI); 35,000 developers; raised $12M from Bessemer and John Schulman. Acquired to accelerate Tab AI model.
- **Koala (~$129M, Jul 2025):** AI CRM acqui-hire; product shut down Sep 2025.
- **Graphite ("way over" $290M, Dec 2025):** Code review platform serving Shopify, Snowflake, Figma, Perplexity. Continues operating independently.

### Poolside Acquires Fern Labs (November 2025) — Confirmed
Poolside AI completed a merger/acquisition with **Fern Labs** on November 18, 2025, expanding its toolchain for enterprise AI coding workflows.

Raised $60M seed at $300M valuation led by Felicis; backers include Jerry Yang, Garry Tan, Olivier Pomel, and M12 (Microsoft's VC arm).

- **Yann LeCun**: Departed Meta (2026) to establish an independent world model research lab; reportedly pursuing a $5B valuation.

- **Scott Breitenother** (Brooklyn Data founder): Co-founded Kilo Code.

- OpenAI responded to talent pressure with up to $1.5M retention bonuses and accelerated vesting.
- Principal AI researchers commanding $1M+/year; superstars negotiating tens of millions.

## Market Context

- AI coding tools collectively raised **$9.4B** between 2022 and 2025, growing from $22M to $7.4B annually. Cursor alone captured >$3.2B of 2025 total across two rounds.
- **Average agentic AI deal size:** $7.4M (2022) → $26.2M (2023) → $109.9M (2024) → $527.8M (2025).
- **AI-native IDEs** captured 38% of all AI code assistant funding ($3.59B across 8 deals), led by Cursor and Windsurf.
- **GitHub Copilot** holds ~42% market share of paid AI coding tools; Cursor ~18%.
- **Sequoia** backed at least four AI coding companies: Magic.dev, Sourcegraph, Factory AI, Zed.
- **YC** backed ~30 of 80 funded AI code assistant startups — most active early-stage pipeline in market.
- Cursor reached $1B ARR in ~17 months — reportedly the fastest B2B SaaS to reach that milestone.
- **Cursor** is now in talks for a ~$50B valuation round, nearly doubling its November 2025 Series D valuation of $29.3B. Bloomberg reported active investor discussions (March 12). Separately, Bloomberg reported Cursor is developing its own AI model to reduce dependency on Anthropic and OpenAI.
- **Lovable** doubled ARR from $200M to $400M in ~4 months (Dec 2025 → Mar 2026) and is now actively pursuing acquisitions.
- **Claude Code** captured ~46% market preference among users adopting agentic coding tools within 8 months of launch.
- **Google** launched **Antigravity**, a full-stack coding agent in AI Studio (multi-session, full project context).
- Employment among software developers aged 22–25 fell ~20% between 2022 and 2025 (Stanford study).
- ~75% of basic coding work can now be completed independently by AI agents (Anthropic Economic Index).
- Global agentic AI market: $9.14B (2026) → projected $139B by 2034.
- The prompt-to-app builder subcategory (Lovable, Bolt.new, Emergent) has attracted nearly $800M in combined funding.
- **Agentic coding security** is emerging as a distinct investment category: Corridor ($25M Series A), Axiom ($200M), Entire ($60M) all targeting governance, safety, or real-time vulnerability management in AI code generation workflows.
