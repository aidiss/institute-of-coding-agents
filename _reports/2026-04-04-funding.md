## Acquisitions & Exits

**Windsurf / Codeium — split outcome (July 2025, ongoing impact)**

The largest structural deal reshaping the competitive landscape heading into 2026:

- OpenAI was in advanced talks to acquire Windsurf for ~$3B; exclusivity expired July 2025 without closing.
- **Google** struck a **$2.4B licensing + talent deal**: CEO Varun Mohan, co-founder Douglas Chen, and ~40 R&D staff joined Google DeepMind's "Antigravity" coding project. Google took no equity stake. *(Confirmed, July 2025)*
- The remaining Windsurf company and product (interim CEO Jeff Wang, President Graham Moreno) was subsequently acquired by **Cognition AI for ~$250M**. *(Confirmed, late 2025)*

**Meta / Dreamer AI — acqui-hire (March 23–24, 2026)**

- Meta licensed Dreamer's technology and hired its entire founding team into Meta Superintelligence Labs. All three co-founders — David Singleton, Hugo Barra (ex-Xiaomi VP), and Nicholas Jitkoff — now report to Chief AI Officer Alexandr Wang.
- Dreamer had raised $56M at a $500M valuation; it was building a personal agent OS with overlapping agentic development capabilities.
- Not a full acquisition of equity; structured as a license + talent deal. *(Confirmed)*

## Context & Themes

- **Platform wars converging**: Cursor 3 (launched April 2, 2026) introduced parallel agent fleets and multi-repo management — directly competing with Claude Code and OpenAI Codex, which launched similar agentic architectures in the same week.
- **Valuation debate**: Newcomer reported investor skepticism about Cursor's trajectory even as headline ARR and valuation targets climb. Growth is real, but sustainability is being questioned.
- **Trust as the next layer**: Axiom raised $200M for verifiable AI code safety, signaling that an "AI code audit" layer is emerging on top of the major coding-agent platforms.
- **Acqui-hire as the dominant M&A structure**: Both the Google/Windsurf and Meta/Dreamer deals were talent-and-license transactions rather than equity acquisitions — a pattern that avoids regulatory scrutiny while capturing key people.
