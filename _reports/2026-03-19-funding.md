## Acquisitions & Exits

### The Windsurf Saga (May–July 2025) — Confirmed
The most complex deal sequence of the year:

1. **OpenAI-Windsurf acquisition agreed (~$3B, May 2025):** OpenAI agreed to acquire Windsurf in what would have been its largest acquisition. Deal collapsed when exclusivity expired.
2. **Google acqui-hires Windsurf founders (Jul 11, 2025, ~$2.4B total):** Google paid ~$1.2B to investors (Greenoaks, which invested $65M and received ~$500M back) and ~$1.2B in compensation to ~40 employees joining Google DeepMind, including co-founders Varun Mohan and Douglas Chen. Google received a non-exclusive technology license — not a full equity acquisition.
3. **Cognition acquires remaining Windsurf entity (~$250M, Jul 14, 2025):** Cognition acquired Windsurf's IP, product, trademark, brand, and remaining staff. Jeff Wang became interim CEO of the Windsurf unit.

### Anthropic Acquires Bun (December 2025) — Confirmed
Anthropic's first-ever acquisition. Bun (JavaScript runtime by Jarred Sumner, 7M monthly downloads) was acquired to own the execution layer underlying Claude Code. Terms undisclosed. Bun remains open-source (MIT license). Announced simultaneously with Claude Code reaching $1B annualized run-rate.

### Anysphere (Cursor) Acquisitions (2025) — Confirmed
Three acquisitions in rapid succession:
- **Koala (~$129M, Jul 2025):** AI-powered CRM startup acqui-hired. Product shut down Sep 2025; select engineers joined to build enterprise capabilities.
- **Growth by Design (Nov 2025):** Tech recruiting strategy company. Terms undisclosed.
- **Graphite ("way over" $290M, Dec 19, 2025):** Code review platform serving 500+ companies including Shopify, Snowflake, Figma, Perplexity. Graphite had raised $52M Series B in March 2025 (Accel, Anthropic's Anthology Fund, Figma Ventures, Shopify Ventures). Graphite continues operating independently.

### Character.AI / Noam Shazeer → Google (~$2.7B, August 2024) — Confirmed
Google licensing/talent deal returned Noam Shazeer (lead "Attention Is All You Need" author) and Daniel De Freitas plus ~30 researchers to Google DeepMind to co-lead Gemini. DOJ and FTC opened investigations into whether such "pseudo-acquisitions" circumvent antitrust review.

## Talent Moves

### Google's "Boomerang" Strategy
- **Noam Shazeer + Daniel De Freitas** (Character.AI co-founders): Part of $2.7B deal (Aug 2024). Both now co-lead Gemini at Google DeepMind.
- **Varun Mohan + Douglas Chen** (Windsurf/Codeium co-founders): Part of $2.4B deal (Jul 2025). Joined Google DeepMind to advance agentic coding and Gemini integration.

### Microsoft
- **Mustafa Suleiman** (DeepMind co-founder): Microsoft paid $650M for Inflection AI (Mar 2024); Suleiman became CEO of Microsoft AI.

### Anthropic
- **Jarred Sumner** (Bun founder): Joined Anthropic following Bun acquisition (Dec 2025). Bun project remains open-source.

### Meta
- **Yann LeCun**: Departed Meta (2026) to establish an independent world model research lab; reportedly pursuing a $5B valuation.

### Anysphere (Cursor)
- **Travis McPeak** (former CEO of cybersecurity startup Resourcely): Hired to lead security team.

### Broader Market Dynamics
- OpenAI responding to talent pressure with up to $1.5M retention bonuses and accelerated vesting.
- Principal AI researchers commanding $1M+/year packages; superstar individuals negotiating tens of millions over multi-year deals.
- Employment among software developers aged 22–25 fell ~20% between 2022 and 2025 (Stanford study) as AI tools displace entry-level coding work.

## Market Context

- AI coding tools market reached **$4–5B in 2025**; projected to reach $12–15B by 2027.
- **GitHub Copilot** holds ~42% market share of paid AI coding tools; **Cursor** ~18% (up from near-zero 18 months prior).
- **Sequoia** backed at least four AI coding companies: Magic.dev, Sourcegraph, Factory AI, Zed.
- **YC** backed ~30 of 80 funded AI code assistant startups — most active early-stage pipeline in the market.
- **OpenAI Codex** (powered by codex-1, a specialized o3 variant): 1M+ weekly active users as of Oct 2025 GA.
