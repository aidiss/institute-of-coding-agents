### Acquisitions & Exits

**Google acqui-hires Windsurf leadership — $2.4B (July 11, 2025, confirmed CNBC)**
Google paid ~$2.4B in licensing fees and compensation to hire Windsurf (Codeium) CEO **Varun Mohan**, co-founder **Douglas Chen**, and ~40 senior R&D staff. Google received a non-exclusive technology license. Mohan and Chen now work within DeepMind on Gemini's agentic coding capabilities. OpenAI had a signed term sheet to acquire Windsurf for ~$3B, but the deal collapsed — reportedly because Microsoft (OpenAI's backer) would have received access to Windsurf's IP. This "Hire And License Out" (HALO) structure is being watched as a model for circumventing full antitrust scrutiny.

**Cognition acquires Windsurf remainder (July 14, 2025, confirmed TechCrunch/CNBC)**
Four days after Google's talent raid, Cognition signed a definitive agreement to acquire Windsurf's remaining IP, product, trademark, brand, and remaining team. All employees received vesting cliff waivers. Jeff Wang became interim Windsurf CEO; Graham Moreno became President. Windsurf regained access to Anthropic Claude models under Cognition. Combined ARR grew 30%+ in first seven weeks post-acquisition.

**Cursor (Anysphere) acquires Supermaven (November 12, 2024, confirmed)**
Undisclosed sum. Supermaven was founded by Jacob Jackson (previously Tabnine co-founder), had raised $12M (Bessemer, John Schulman of OpenAI, Denis Yarats of Perplexity). 35,000+ developer signups at time of acquisition. Brought ultra-fast long-context code completion (Babble model) into Cursor Tab.

**Cursor acquires Graphite (December 2025, confirmed Fortune)**
Code review startup acquired by Cursor. Terms undisclosed.

**Anthropic acquires Bun (December 2025)**
Anthropic acquired the Bun JavaScript runtime (4–8x faster than Node.js). Rationale: Claude Code ships as a Bun executable. Claude Code hit $1B annualized run rate after its May 2025 public launch.

**Poolside acquires Fern Labs (November 2025)**
Terms undisclosed.

**Meta acqui-hires Dreamer AI (March 24, 2026)**
Dreamer AI (personal agent OS for non-technical users; had raised $56M at $500M valuation) was acqui-hired by Meta for its Superintelligence Labs division. Part of Meta's aggressive AI talent acquisition pattern since mid-2025 (also includes Scale AI/Alexandr Wang, June 2025).

## Key Themes

1. **Cursor's model independence bet:** Cursor launched Composer 2 in March 2026, its first proprietary coding model (built on Kimi K2.5 base, fine-tuned for coding). Benchmarks: 61.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 vs Claude Opus 4.6 at 58.0%. Cost: $0.50/M input tokens (~86% cheaper than prior model). Context: Cursor competes directly with Anthropic and OpenAI while depending on their models — unsustainable long-term economics.

2. **HALO acquisitions:** Google's $2.4B Windsurf talent deal is being closely watched as a template. Hire the team + license the IP = significant talent acquisition without triggering full antitrust merger review.

3. **Vibe-coding breakout:** Lovable ($200M+ ARR) and Replit ($265M ARR, 1,556% YoY growth) validated the no-code/low-code AI segment at scale.

4. **Consolidation accelerating:** Cursor acquired Supermaven + Graphite; Cognition absorbed Windsurf; Poolside acquired Fern Labs; Anthropic acquired Bun; Meta acqui-hired Dreamer AI — all within a ~16-month window.

5. **Lab spinouts commanding extraordinary capital:** Murati's Thinking Machines Lab ($2B seed), Humans& ($480M seed), Isara ($650M valuation) — the pedigree premium has never been higher.
