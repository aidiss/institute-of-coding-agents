# Releases — 2026-04-05

What shipped this week across models, products, and open source.

## Models

**Google Gemma 4** — Released April 2, 2026 (GA, Apache 2.0)
Four open-weight model sizes: E2B (2.3B effective), E4B (4.5B effective), 26B-A4B MoE (4B active), and 31B dense. Built on Gemini 3 architecture with up to 256K token context windows, native vision and audio, and support for 140+ languages. Marks a change to Apache 2.0 licensing. Available on Google Cloud, Hugging Face, and via Android AICore Developer Preview.

**Google Gemini 3.1 Pro & Flash-Live** — Rolling out globally (Flash-Live in preview)
Gemini 3.1 Pro targets complex reasoning and data synthesis; rolling out via the Gemini app globally with higher rate limits on AI Pro and Ultra plans. Gemini 3.1 Flash-Live is an audio-to-audio model for real-time voice dialogue, available in 200+ countries via Search Live and Gemini Live.

**Google Veo 3.1 Lite** — Early April 2026 (Preview)
Google's most cost-efficient video generation model. Supports Text-to-Video and Image-to-Video in landscape and portrait at 720p and 1080p. Pricing reduction announced for April 7.

**Alibaba Qwen 3.6-Plus** — Released April 2, 2026 (GA, proprietary)
1 million token context window by default. Tops tested models on SWE-bench Verified (78.8) and Terminal-Bench 2.0 (61.6). Enhanced multimodal reasoning. Available on Alibaba's Bailian platform at ~$0.29/M input, ~$1.74/M output. Marks a shift from Alibaba's prior open-weight approach.

**Alibaba Qwen 3.5-Omni** — Released March 30, 2026 (GA)
Unified multimodal model processing text, images, audio, and video in a single pipeline. Speech recognition across 113 languages/dialects; speech generation in 36.

**xAI Grok 4.20 Beta 2** — Late March 2026 (Beta)
Current xAI flagship iteration. Fixes improved instruction following, reduced hallucinations, enhanced LaTeX support, more accurate image search, and better multi-image rendering. Grok 5 (reportedly a 6T-parameter MoE) remains in training.

**Zhipu AI GLM-5V-Turbo** — Released April 2, 2026
Multimodal vision model from Chinese lab Zhipu AI.

## Open Source

**Netflix VOID (Video Object and Interaction Deletion)** — Released April 3–4, 2026 (Apache 2.0)
First public open-source AI model from Netflix. Removes objects from video footage and physically plausibly regenerates what remains — e.g., removing a person from a pool scene and showing an undisturbed pool surface. Built on CogVideoX with a two-pass transformer pipeline. Training data generated via Blender physics re-simulation (HUMOTO) and Google's Kubric framework. In human preference tests preferred over Runway Gen-3 at 64.8% vs 18.4%. Available on Hugging Face at `netflix/void-model`.

**OpenHands V1 SDK** — April 2026 (open source)
V0 formally deprecated. V1 moves from mandatory Docker to optional sandboxing with `LocalWorkspace` as the default for lower friction. Model-agnostic, event-sourced state with deterministic replay, and MCP integration. Presented at MLSys 2026. Main repo at ~69,500 GitHub stars.

**Aider** — Rolling updates (March–April 2026)
Added Grok Code Fast 1 support, Responses API support for o1-pro and o3-pro, `--add-gitignore-files` flag, Knight Rider spinner animation, and updated Gemini 2.5 model aliases.
