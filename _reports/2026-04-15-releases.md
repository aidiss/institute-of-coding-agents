# Releases — 2026-04-15

What shipped? New model releases, product launches, and major updates across the AI coding agent landscape.

## Models

### Google Gemma 4 — GA, April 2, 2026
Google released Gemma 4, described as its most capable open model family to date. The family ships four variants: Effective 2B (E2B), Effective 4B (E4B), 26B Mixture-of-Experts, and a 31B Dense model. Key specs: up to 256K context window, native vision and audio, 140+ language support. Built off Gemini 3 internals. Released under the Apache 2.0 license (a change from prior Gemma custom licenses that blocked enterprise use). Available on Google Cloud, Hugging Face, and Android AICore Developer Preview. Benchmarks show the 31B model outperforming models 20× its size on several tasks.

**Sources:** [Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/gemma-4/) · [Google Cloud Blog](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/gemma-4-available-on-google-cloud) · [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/ai/google-releases-gemma-4-a-family-of-open-models-built-off-of-gemini-3-160000332.html)

### Meta Llama 4 Scout & Maverick — Open-weight, April 5, 2026
Meta released two open-weight multimodal models. **Llama 4 Maverick** is a 17B active-parameter, 128-expert MoE model (~400B total params) with a 1M-token context window on the instruct variant. **Llama 4 Scout** is a smaller companion model. Both are natively multimodal and available for download on Hugging Face and llama.com. Maverick claimed best-in-class on multimodal benchmarks at release, beating GPT-4o and Gemini 2.0 Flash on a range of tasks.

**Sources:** [Meta AI Blog](https://ai.meta.com/blog/llama-4-multimodal-intelligence/) · [Llama.com](https://www.llama.com/models/llama-4/) · [Hugging Face — Maverick](https://huggingface.co/meta-llama/Llama-4-Maverick-17B-128E-Instruct)

### Meta Muse Spark — Closed/hosted, April 8, 2026
Meta's first proprietary frontier model released with closed, hosted-only access. No open weights. Positions Meta as a dual-track player (open-weight Llama 4 + proprietary frontier).

**Sources:** [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)

### Anthropic — Claude Mythos withheld, announced April 7, 2026
Anthropic announced that its most powerful model (leaked as "Claude Mythos") will **not** be publicly released due to assessed cybersecurity risks. It will be made available only to select partners under **Project Glasswing**. Not a release — notable as a deliberate non-release amid broad capability concerns.

**Sources:** [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates) · [WhatLLM](https://whatllm.org/blog/new-ai-models-april-2026)

## Products

### Cursor 3 — GA, April 2, 2026
Anysphere shipped Cursor 3 with a fully agent-first interface. Key new capabilities: parallel background cloud agents, local-to-cloud agent handoff, Design Mode, BugBot, Ghost Mode, and multi-pane parallel agent windows. Pricing unchanged. Composer 2 (Anysphere's in-house frontier coding model, built on a Kimi K2.5 base with a 4× RL run) shipped March 19 and is bundled into Cursor 3; it scores 61.3 on CursorBench vs. 44.2 for Composer 1.5 (~39% improvement) at 200+ tok/s.

**Sources:** [cursor.com/blog/cursor-3](https://cursor.com/blog/cursor-3) · [DataCamp](https://www.datacamp.com/blog/cursor-3) · [Medium](https://medium.com/@tentenco/cursor-3-ships-an-agent-first-interface-heres-what-it-actually-changes-1f2bf8f383e2)

## Open Source

### OpenHands v1.6.0 — March 30, 2026 (most recent heading into this week)
