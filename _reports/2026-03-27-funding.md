## Acquisitions & Exits

### Anthropic acquires Vercept — February 25, 2026
Anthropic acqui-hired Seattle-based **Vercept** (total raised: ~$50M), a computer-use AI startup whose Vy agent operated MacBooks via natural language. Co-founders Kiana Ehsani, Luca Weihs, and Ross Girshick joined Anthropic to advance Claude's agentic computer-use capabilities. Vy app shut down 30 days post-acquisition. Notably, co-founder Matt Deitke had already left for Meta (see Talent Moves) before the deal closed.

### Cognition AI acquires Windsurf — ~December 2025
After a collapsed $3B OpenAI acquisition and a $2.4B Google talent/licensing deal that paid out founders and VCs, Windsurf was acquired by Cognition AI for approximately **$250M** (rumored; not officially confirmed). At acquisition, Windsurf had $82M ARR and 350+ enterprise customers. The Windsurf brand continues under Cognition, with Devin's autonomous agent capabilities being integrated into the IDE.

## Talent Moves

### xAI poaches four senior Cursor executives — March 12–13, 2026
xAI recruited **Andrew Milich** and **Jason Ginsberg** (senior product/engineering leads who helped scale Cursor to $2B ARR), plus **Brandon Borzelli** (head of revenue) and **Tushar Makhija** (head of business operations). Milich and Ginsberg will report directly to Elon Musk. xAI is scrapping its prior coding tool and rebuilding from scratch (internal project name reportedly "Macrohard"), with a mid-2026 target to compete. This follows mass co-founder departures at xAI — six of twelve co-founders have left since January 2026 (Toby Pohlen, Jimmy Ba, Tony Wu, Greg Yang, Zihang Dai, Guodong Zhang).

### Meta poaches Vercept co-founder Matt Deitke — February 2026
Computer vision researcher and Vercept co-founder (formerly Allen Institute for AI) Matt Deitke joined Meta's Superintelligence Lab for a reported **$250M compensation package**, departing before the rest of Vercept was acquired by Anthropic.

### Meta poaches Tim Brooks from Google DeepMind — Early 2026
Tim Brooks (co-led Sora video model team at OpenAI, then DeepMind) joined Meta's Superintelligence Labs, signaling Meta's push into world models.

### Yann LeCun departs Meta — Early 2026
LeCun, Meta's longtime chief AI scientist, left to start his own "world model lab," reportedly seeking a $5B valuation.

## New Startups

### Entire — February 2026
Former GitHub CEO **Nat Friedman** launched **Entire**, a developer platform for human-AI collaboration built around Git. Raised $60M seed at $300M valuation, led by Thrive Capital; angels include GitHub co-founder Tom Preston-Werner and former Stripe CTO David Singleton. Core product: an open-source CLI that extends Git to record AI agent session history (prompts, tool calls, token usage) as structured metadata alongside commits.
