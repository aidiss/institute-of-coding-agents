# Funding: AI Coding Agent Space
*Report date: 2026-04-01*

## Overview

2026 is on pace to be a record year for AI coding agent investment. The sector collectively raised over **$3B** across seed-through-late-stage rounds, with average round sizes nearly doubling vs. H1 2025 ($155M vs. $82M). Macro context: Anthropic closed a $30B Series G in February 2026 at a $380B post-money valuation, and global venture investment hit $189B in February — the largest startup funding month ever recorded.

## Acquisitions & Exits

### Windsurf — Three-Way Split (July 2025)

The most dramatic deal in the space. Windsurf (formerly Codeium, formerly Exofunction) was caught in a billion-dollar tug-of-war between three AI giants over 72 hours:

1. **OpenAI** announced a **$3B acquisition** of Windsurf in April 2025. The deal collapsed when Microsoft — which holds IP rights over OpenAI's acquisitions per their partnership — refused to grant an exception to shield Windsurf IP from GitHub Copilot's competitor. Deal died July 11, 2025.

2. **Google** executed a **$2.4B reverse acquihire** days later, hiring CEO Varun Mohan, co-founder Douglas Chen, and senior researchers via a licensing-not-acquisition structure. Criticism followed over employees hired within the last year reportedly being excluded from financial participation.

3. **Cognition** acquired the remaining ~210-person team, all IP, product, brand, and trademark at an undisclosed price (believed well below the $2.85B prior tag). Cognition waived all vesting cliffs for inherited employees. Deal announced July 14, 2025.

**Outcome:** Cognition now holds both Devin (autonomous agent) and Windsurf IDE (developer workspace) — a unified platform for both collaborative and autonomous coding workflows. Cognition was valued at $10.2B by September 2025.

### Poolside — Fern Labs Acquisition

Poolside (AI coding model startup, $3B Series B) acquired **Fern Labs** in November 2025. Deal terms undisclosed.

### Broader Trends

- **Chief AI Officer (CAIO)** is now present at 1 in 4 large companies; 66% of executives expect most companies to hire one within two years.
- Demand for **AI agent architects** — professionals who can build multi-agent systems into production — is outstripping supply almost entirely. The discipline barely existed two years ago.
- AI skills command a **56% pay premium** over equivalent non-AI roles; AI agent developers at the 90th percentile earn $232K (as of March 2026).
- GitHub Copilot now writes ~40% of code in files where it runs, reducing demand for junior developers focused on routine CRUD work.
