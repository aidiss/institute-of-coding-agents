## Acquisitions & Exits

### Windsurf (Codeium) — Three-Way Split (July 2025) ✓ Confirmed

The defining M&A event of the period. Windsurf had $82M ARR, 350+ enterprise clients, 1M+ developers at time of sale.

**OpenAI deal — collapsed (~$3B):** OpenAI agreed in late April 2025 to acquire Windsurf for ~$3B — its largest acquisition ever. Collapsed July 2025 over a Microsoft IP dispute: Microsoft asserted its partnership rights extended to Windsurf's technology; OpenAI refused.

**Google reverse acqui-hire — $2.4B:** Immediately upon expiry of OpenAI exclusivity (July 11, 2025), Google paid $2.4B to bring CEO Varun Mohan, co-founder Douglas Chen, and ~40 engineers into DeepMind for Gemini agentic coding. Split ~$1.2B to investors, ~$1.2B in compensation packages.

**Cognition acquires the remainder — ~$250M:** Within 72 hours of the Google announcement, Cognition acquired Windsurf's remaining ~210 employees, product, brand, and $82M ARR business. All Windsurf employees received equity, eliminating typical vesting cliffs.

### Anysphere (Cursor) — Supermaven Acquisition (November 2024) ✓ Confirmed

Acquired Supermaven (founded by Jacob Jackson, co-founder of Tabnine; $12M raised from Bessemer, John Schulman, Denis Yarats). Added Supermaven's Babble model (high-context, low-latency completions) to Cursor Tab AI.

---

### Anysphere (Cursor) — Graphite Acquisition (December 2025) ✓ Confirmed

Acquired Graphite, a code review platform that had raised a $52M Series B at ~$290M valuation in 2025.

---

### Microsoft / GitHub — Structural Exit (August 2025) ✓ Confirmed

Microsoft eliminated the standalone GitHub CEO role and folded GitHub into its CoreAI organization under Jay Parikh. Not a third-party acquisition, but effectively ended GitHub's independent operating posture. GitHub Copilot had 20M users and was in 90% of Fortune 100 companies at the time.
