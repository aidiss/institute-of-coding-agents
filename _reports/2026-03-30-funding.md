## Acquisitions & Exits

### Meta — Dreamer AI Acqui-hire (March 23–24, 2026) ✓ Confirmed

Meta's Superintelligence Labs (led by Chief AI Officer Alexandr Wang) acqui-hired all three co-founders of Dreamer AI: **David Singleton**, **Hugo Barra**, and **Nicholas Jitkoff**. Dreamer had raised $56M at a $500M valuation and was building a personal agent OS for non-technical users. Deal terms undisclosed; Meta obtained a non-exclusive technology license. Dreamer remains a standalone legal entity.

## Context & Trends

**Cursor model controversy:** On March 19–21, Cursor launched Composer 2, its own coding-specific model priced at $0.50/M input tokens. The model was built on the Chinese open-source Kimi K2.5, which was not initially disclosed, sparking controversy. On Terminal-Bench 2.0 it scores 61.7%, edging Claude Opus 4.6 at 58.0%.

**Market scale:** The AI coding tools market hit ~$4–5B in 2025; projected $12–15B by 2027. March 2026 set a record for the number of $100M+ AI funding rounds in any comparable 2.5-week stretch (SiliconANGLE, March 13, 2026).

**Valuation premium:** "Agentic" labeled startups are raising seed rounds at 40% higher valuations than "Generative AI" labeled peers.

**Consolidation signal:** Meta, OpenAI, and Google all made talent moves in Q1 2026. Factory, Codegen, and Wrap remain cited as likely 2026 acquisition targets.
