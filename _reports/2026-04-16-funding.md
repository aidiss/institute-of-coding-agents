## Acquisitions & Exits

### OpenAI — Astral (March 19, 2026) ✓ Confirmed

OpenAI agreed to acquire **Astral**, creator of the widely used open-source Python toolchain: **uv** (package installer), **Ruff** (linter), and **ty** (type checker). Astral's tools underpin millions of developer workflows. Terms undisclosed. The Astral team will join OpenAI's **Codex** team and work toward deeper integration of runtime tooling with Codex's agentic development capabilities.

---

### OpenAI — Promptfoo (March 2026) ✓ Confirmed

OpenAI acquired **Promptfoo**, an open-source framework for red-teaming and testing AI applications. Focused on security and reliability evaluation of LLM outputs. Terms undisclosed. This is OpenAI's sixth acquisition in 2026 — nearly matching its full-year 2025 count of eight.

## Context & Trends

**OpenAI M&A velocity:** Six acquisitions in 2026 through March, nearly matching all of 2025 (eight deals). Focus areas: open-source developer tooling (Astral, Promptfoo), talent from the agentic coding ecosystem (Cline engineers, Peter Steinberger/OpenClaw). FTC Chair Andrew Ferguson announced an investigation into AI acqui-hires in January 2026.

**Codex resurgence:** Reuters reported in April 2026 that competitive pressure from Anthropic's Claude Code led OpenAI to redirect resources toward Codex. The Astral and Promptfoo acquisitions, combined with the OpenClaw hire, signal a concerted effort to build a full-stack developer platform around Codex — from runtime tools to agent infrastructure.

**Code verification as a category:** Qodo's $70M Series B signals enterprise appetite for a distinct "code quality and governance" layer on top of AI-generated code. As AI coding agents write more production code, verification tooling is emerging as a standalone market segment.

**Valuation compression risk:** Anysphere's $60B target (11× forward ARR at $2B run rate) and Lovable's $6.6B (33× ARR at $200M) illustrate the divergence in how the market prices productivity vs. growth-stage tools. Skeptics note that Cursor faces model commoditization risk as frontier labs ship native coding agents.
