## Acquisitions & Exits

### Windsurf — Three-Way Split (Jul 2025)

One of the most dramatic transactions in the AI coding space unfolded in 72 hours in July 2025:

1. **OpenAI's $3B acquisition fell apart** after a dispute with Microsoft over IP rights. Microsoft expected Windsurf's technology to fall under its existing OpenAI partnership rights; OpenAI refused.
2. **Google / Windsurf — $2.4B talent + license deal (Confirmed).** Google DeepMind hired CEO Varun Mohan, co-founder Douglas Chen, and ~40 top researchers. Google received a non-exclusive technology license. Notably, Google took no equity stake, structuring the deal to avoid full-acquisition regulatory review. Investor payout: ~$1.2B. Employee comp (founders + ~40 hires): ~$1.2B. Windsurf had raised ~$243M total at a $1.25B valuation prior to the deal (~4× return for VCs including Greenoaks, Kleiner Perkins, General Catalyst).
3. **Cognition (Devin) acquires Windsurf's remaining entity — ~$250M (Confirmed).** Cognition acquired Windsurf's IP, product, trademark, and brand within days of the Google deal. Roughly 210 of Windsurf's ~250 employees — who did not join Google — came with the acquisition.

### OpenAI / Convogo — Acqui-hire (Jan 2026, Confirmed)

OpenAI hired the team behind Convogo, a business software platform, to work on "AI cloud efforts." OpenAI did not acquire Convogo's IP or technology. Marks OpenAI's ninth acquisition in a year (per PitchBook).

### Microsoft / Robin AI — Acqui-hire (Jan 2026, Reported)

Microsoft is reportedly acquiring the tech team of Robin AI (legal AI) to improve Word for lawyers. Team size estimated at several dozen. Deal not formally confirmed at time of reporting.

## Talent Moves

### Alibaba Qwen AI — Leadership Exodus (Jan–Mar 2026)

Three senior technical leaders departed Alibaba's Qwen AI division within ten weeks, directly impacting its Qwen Code coding initiative:

- **Hui Binyuan** (Staff Research Scientist, lead of Qwen Code) → joined **Meta** (Jan 2026). Emblematic of Meta's aggressive cross-border recruitment of Chinese AI talent.
- **Lin Junyang ("Justin Lin")** (Qwen model lead) — announced resignation on X on Mar 3, 2026, one day after Alibaba unified all AI products under the Qwen brand.
- **Yu Bowen** (Head of Post-Training, Qwen) — also resigned the same week, per Chinese media reports. Post-training (RLHF, instruction tuning, alignment) is considered the most strategically sensitive phase of model development.

Qwen Code had been positioned as a direct competitor in the agentic coding space. The departures raise questions about the project's continuity and Alibaba's ability to retain frontier AI talent against Meta, ByteDance, Baidu, Moonshot AI, and DeepSeek.

### OpenClaw / OpenAI (Early 2026)

Peter Steinberger, creator of the open-source **OpenClaw** agent, joined OpenAI's **Codex team** after every major AI lab reportedly attempted to recruit him. Signals that labs view personal agent orchestration as a strategic control layer, not just a product feature.

### Windsurf → Google DeepMind (Jul 2025)

- **Varun Mohan** (CEO, Windsurf) → Google DeepMind, Gemini division
- **Douglas Chen** (Co-founder, Windsurf) → Google DeepMind, Gemini division
- ~38 additional Windsurf R&D staff → Google DeepMind

## Context

The AI coding agent funding market remains one of the hottest categories in venture. AI startups raised a record $238B in total funding during 2025 (47% of all VC activity), with Q1 2026 opening at $13B+ in January alone. Acqui-hires have become the dominant M&A structure for talent-intensive AI deals, allowing buyers to avoid full-acquisition regulatory scrutiny — a pattern the FTC and DOJ have begun to investigate.
