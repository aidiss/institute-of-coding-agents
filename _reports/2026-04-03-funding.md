## Acquisitions & Exits

### Google acqui-hires Windsurf (Codeium) — $2.4B (confirmed, Jul 11 2025)
Google executed a "HALO" (Hire And License Out) deal hours after OpenAI's acquisition exclusivity expired. Structure: ~$1.2B to Windsurf investors + ~$1.2B in compensation to ~40 engineers joining Google DeepMind. CEO Varun Mohan and co-founder/CTO Douglas Chen now lead Gemini agentic coding. Google received a non-exclusive technology license, not equity or full IP. Previously, OpenAI had signed a ~$3B term sheet (largest proposed acquisition ever) that collapsed when Microsoft asserted its OpenAI partnership entitled it to Windsurf's IP.

### Cognition acquires remaining Windsurf — ~$250M (confirmed, Jul 14 2025)
Three days after the Google deal, Cognition (Devin) acquired Windsurf's IP, product, brand, trademarks, and ~210 remaining employees. All employees received vesting cliff waivers. Windsurf regained access to Claude models. Combined ARR grew 30%+ in the first seven weeks post-close; ~30 employees were later laid off and ~200 offered buyouts (9 months salary).

### OpenAI acquires Astral — undisclosed (confirmed, Mar 19 2026)
Astral is the Python developer tooling startup founded by Charlie Marsh. Its three open-source tools — uv (dependency management), Ruff (Rust-based linter/formatter), and ty (type enforcement) — collectively see hundreds of millions of downloads per month. All 30+ employees including Marsh join OpenAI's Codex division. Tools remain open-source. Pending regulatory close. Structurally mirrors Anthropic's Bun acquisition.

### Anthropic acquires Bun — undisclosed (confirmed, Dec 2025)
Anthropic's first-ever acquisition. Bun (JavaScript runtime, 7M monthly downloads, 82K GitHub stars) was acquired because Claude Code ships as a single Bun executable. Bun creator Jarred Sumner joined Anthropic and continues leading the project. Bun remains open-source (MIT). Announced alongside Claude Code reaching $1B annualized run rate within ~6 months of its May 2025 launch.

### Anthropic acqui-hires Vercept — undisclosed (confirmed, Feb 25 2026)
Seattle-based computer-use AI startup (total raised: ~$50M). Co-founders Kiana Ehsani, Luca Weihs, and Ross Girshick joined Anthropic to advance Claude's agentic computer-use capabilities. The Vy app was shut down within 30 days post-acquisition. Note: co-founder Matt Deitke had already departed for Meta (reported ~$250M comp) before the deal closed.

### Anysphere (Cursor) acquires Graphite — reported "way over" $290M (confirmed, Dec 2025)
Graphite is a code review platform serving Shopify, Snowflake, Figma, and Perplexity. Had raised $52M Series B (Accel, Anthropic's Anthology Fund). Continues operating independently post-acquisition. Cursor also acquired Koala (~$129M, Jul 2025, AI CRM acqui-hire, product shut Sep 2025) and Supermaven (Nov 2024, AI code-completion model).

### Meta acqui-hires Dreamer AI (confirmed, Mar 23–24 2026)
Meta's Superintelligence Labs (led by Alexandr Wang) brought on all three Dreamer AI co-founders: David Singleton, Hugo Barra, and Nicholas Jitkoff. Dreamer had raised $56M at a $500M valuation and built a personal agent OS. Meta received a non-exclusive technology license; Dreamer remains a standalone legal entity.

### Microsoft folds GitHub into CoreAI (confirmed, Aug 2025)
Microsoft eliminated the standalone GitHub CEO role and integrated GitHub into its CoreAI division under Jay Parikh. GitHub Copilot had 20M users and was in 90% of Fortune 100 at the time. CEO Thomas Dohmke resigned (later founded Entire, Feb 2026).

### OpenAI acqui-hires Cline engineers (confirmed, Jan 2026)
At least 7 engineers from the Cline open-source coding assistant (5M+ VS Code installs) joined OpenAI's Codex team. Cline clarified it remains an independent company.

### Poolside acquires Fern Labs (confirmed, Nov 18 2025)
Terms undisclosed.

## Market Context

- **AI coding tools** raised $9.4B collectively between 2022 and 2025, growing from $22M/year to $7.4B/year. Average agentic AI deal size rose from $7.4M (2022) to $527.8M (2025). AI-native IDEs captured 38% of all AI code assistant funding.
- **Cursor's proprietary model (Composer 2, Mar 19–21 2026):** Built on the Chinese open-source Kimi K2.5 base (not disclosed initially, causing controversy). Priced at $0.50/M input tokens (~86% cheaper than prior). Scored 61.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 vs. Claude Opus 4.6 at 58.0%. Signals Cursor's intent to reduce dependency on Anthropic and OpenAI model providers.
- **YC W26 Demo Day (Mar 26 2026):** ~190 companies; 14 hit $1M ARR by Demo Day — a YC record. Coding-adjacent standouts: CodeWisp (AI game building), Approxima (autonomous bug-fix agents).
