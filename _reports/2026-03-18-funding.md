### Acquisitions & Exits

**Google / Windsurf (acqui-hire, ~$2.4B total — Jul 2025) — Confirmed**
Google structured a talent + IP license deal — not an equity acquisition — to hire Windsurf CEO Varun Mohan, co-founder Douglas Chen, and ~40 engineers. Deal split roughly 50/50: ~$1.2B to investors and ~$1.2B in employee compensation. Windsurf's remaining corporate entity was not acquired by Google; it was subsequently sold to Cognition.

**Cognition / Windsurf (~$250M — Dec 2025) — Confirmed**
After the Google talent deal, Cognition acquired Windsurf's remaining business and brand. The Windsurf IDE continues under Cognition, integrating Devin capabilities. This roughly doubled Cognition's ARR.

**Anthropic / Bun (JavaScript runtime) — Reported**
Anthropic acquired Bun to secure the infrastructure underpinning Claude Code, which ships as a Bun executable. Financial terms not disclosed.

**ClickUp / Codegen (Dec 23, 2025) — Confirmed**
Terms not disclosed. Codegen CEO Jay Hack joined ClickUp as Head of AI; embeds AI coding agents into ClickUp workflows.

**OpenAI / OpenClaw (acqui-hire, early 2026) — Confirmed**
OpenAI hired Peter Steinberger, creator of the open-source personal agent platform OpenClaw, to join its Codex team. Meta, Anthropic, and xAI also competed for Steinberger. OpenClaw moved to a newly formed foundation (Dave Morin on board).

### New Startups by Notable Founders

**Entire** — Thomas Dohmke (ex-GitHub CEO)
Governance and auditability platform for agent-generated code. First product: Checkpoints, an open-source CLI tool. $60M seed at $300M valuation (largest seed round ever for a dev tools startup, per reports). 15 employees as of Feb 2026.

**Kilo Code** — Sid Sijbrandij (GitLab co-founder) + Scott Breitenother (ex-Brooklyn Data)
Open-source AI coding agent for VS Code, JetBrains, and CLI. 1.5M+ users, 25T+ tokens processed, #1 coding agent on OpenRouter. $8M seed.
